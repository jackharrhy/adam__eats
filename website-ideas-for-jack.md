# Creamy Cajun Chicken Pasta:

Creamy Cajun Chicken pasta\! 🍝\
Ingredient\
8 oz linguine pasta\
4 boneless chicken thighs cut into small pieces\
2 tsp olive oil\
4 Tbsp unsalted butter\
1 1/2 to 2 Tbsp cajun seasoning\
5 garlic cloves, minced\
1 Tbsp tomato paste\
1 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream\
1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese\
2 Tbsp parsley, finely chopped, to serve

Fry seasoned chicken in pan until cooked. Remove from the pan and place in a
bowl to the side\
Add butter to the same pan and sauté garlic until aromatic. Add tomato paste and
fry for one minute\
Add in the heavy whipping cream, add cajun seasoning to taste, and parmesan
cheese, and bring to a simmer\
Add the cooked chicken and cooked pasta to the sauce and toss until combined and
pasta and chicken are warmed through. Add warm reserved pasta water if desired
to thin the sauce. Serve with freshly grated parmesan and chopped parsley

# Chocolate Chip Pecan Cookies

Ingredients

1.25 cups (280g) unsalted browned butter\
1 lb (450g) roughly chopped dark chocolate\
1.5 cups (230g) cake flour\
2 cups (275g) all-purpose flour\
1.5 tsp (5g) kosher salt\
2 tsp (8g) cornstarch\
½ tsp (6g) baking soda\
1.25 cups (285g) light brown sugar\
½ cup (115g) granulated sugar\
2 large eggs\
3 large egg yolks\
2tsp (10g) vanilla extract\
2 cups (165g) roughly chopped pecans\
Directions

In a medium saucepan, melt the butter and then set aside to cool.\
In a medium bowl, whisk together the flours, salt, cornstarch, and baking soda.
Set aside.\
In a medium bowl whisk together the light brown sugar and granulated sugar with
the melted butter. Continue to whisk until the mixture is creamy.\
Next, add in the eggs one at a time followed by the egg yolks, making sure to
mix each egg fully into the batter before adding the next.\
Whisk in vanilla extract\
Once smooth, mix in the flour mixture until fully incorporated.\
Fold in the pecans and chocolate until combined.\
Place the batter in a bowl, cover, and refrigerate for at least an hour to chill
completely.\
Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).\
Divide the dough into around 6 ounce pieces. Roll each piece into a tightly
packed ball.\
Place 4 cookies on a parchment lined baking sheet. Repeat with the remaining
dough.\
Bake for 10-13 minutes, or until lightly browned on the outside and slightly
underdone on the inside.\
Set on a wire rack to cool slightly before serving.

# Sticky Toffee Pudding

- # Sticky Toffee Pudding\! A great dessert to make during the holiday season\!🎄🎅

- # Cake:

- 1 cup (155g) pitted dates, roughly chopped\
  1 cup (240mL) water, boiling\
  ½ tsp (2g) baking soda\
  1.5 cups (225g) all-purpose flour\
  ¼ tsp (\~1g) baking powder\
  ¾ tsp (4g) fine sea salt\
  ½ tsp (\~1g) nutmeg, freshly grated\
  6 Tbsp (84g) unsalted butter, softened, plus more for greasing\
  ¾ cup (150g) light brown sugar, packed\
  2 large eggs, at room temperature\
  1 large egg yolk, at room temperature\
  2 tsp (12g) vanilla bean paste or vanilla extract (I used one vanilla bean)\
  Toffee Sauce:\
  1 cup (200g) light brown sugar, packed\
  ½ cup (112g) unsalted butter\
  ⅓ cup (80mL) light corn syrup\
  2 Tbsp (30mL) molasses\
  2 cups (480mL) heavy whipping cream\
  1 tsp (6g) vanilla bean paste or vanilla extract (I used one vanilla bean)\
  Generous pinch of fine sea salt\
  Directions\
  Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C) and grease a 9x13” baking dish with
  butter.\
  Bring dates and water to a boil over medium high heat in a small pot. Once
  boiling, reduce heat and simmer until soft. Add your baking soda and puree
  until smooth.\
  In a bowl combine flour, baking powder, fine sea salt, nutmeg, and salt. Whisk
  until combined. In a separate large bowl add your softened butter and whisk
  until creamy. Next, add your light brown sugar and cream and beat until light
  and fluffy, about 3-5 minutes. Then, add in your eggs (1 at a time), egg yolk,
  vanilla bean paste, and date puree. Fold in half of your flour mixture and mix
  until just combined. Repeat with the other half of your flour mixture.\
  Pour your cake batter into your baking dish and bake for 25-30 minutes or
  until you can poke the center of the cake with a cake tester and it comes out
  clean. Once baking is complete, immediately poke holes into the cake using a
  wooden skewer or fork.\
  In a sauce pan, combine the light brown sugar, corn syrup, molasses, and
  butter. Then add 1 cup of your cream. Bring to a boil and cook for about 4
  minutes. Reduce heat and cook and reduce for 15-20 minutes or until a deep
  amber caramel forms. Add remaining cream and cook for 3-4 more minutes.\
  Pour sauce over cake, slice, and serve with ice-cream\!

# Chocolate Mousse

Chocolate Cake Layer

Ingredients

1 cup (115g) flour

½ tsp (2–3g) baking powder

½ tsp (3g) baking soda

2 heaping tbsp (30g) unsweetened cocoa powder

¾ cup (150g) sugar

Pinch of salt

1 large egg

¼ cup (60ml) vegetable oil

½ cup (120ml) milk

½ cup (120ml) hot water

Directions

Sift flour, baking powder, baking soda, and cocoa into a bowl. Add sugar and
salt.

In another bowl, whisk together egg, oil, and milk.

Combine wet and dry ingredients. Gradually stir in hot water until smooth and
runny.

Pour into a greased 9” springform pan lined with parchment paper.

Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25–30 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.

Cool completely before adding syrup.

Coffee Syrup

Ingredients

¼ cup (60ml) water

2 tbsp (25g) sugar

1 tsp instant coffee

Directions

Combine all ingredients in a small saucepan.

Bring to a boil, then remove from heat.

Brush evenly over the cooled cake.

Chocolate Mousse

Ingredients

7 oz (200g) dark chocolate (70%)

1⅓ cups (320ml) heavy cream (for ganache)

1 cup (240ml) cold heavy cream (for whipping)

1 tsp (5g) vanilla

1 tbsp (12g) sugar

Directions

Heat 1⅓ cups of cream to about 150°F (65°C).

Pour over chopped chocolate and let sit for 1 minute. Stir until smooth.

Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for 30–40 minutes.

Whisk the chilled chocolate mixture until light and fluffy.

In another bowl, whip the remaining cream with sugar and vanilla to soft peaks.

Gently fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture.

Spread mousse evenly over the cake and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set.

Chocolate Mirror Glaze

Ingredients

½ cup (125g) water

1 cup (195g) sugar

¼ cup (35g) cocoa powder

7g gelatin

1 tbsp (22g) glucose or corn syrup

Directions

Bring water, sugar, and syrup to a boil.

Sift cocoa into a bowl, then whisk in the hot syrup until smooth.

Add gelatin and stir until fully dissolved.

Cool glaze to 30°C (86°F).

Pour over the chilled mousse cake, letting it spread evenly.

Allow glaze to set before slicing and serving.

# Buljol

Recipe\
Ingredients for buljol:\
1 pound of salt cod fish\
3 tbsp of any kind of acid (lemon/lime juice or vinegar)\
1 medium onion\
4 garlic cloves\
1 habanero pepper with the seeds removed\
4-5 tbsp of olive oil\
Avocado, bell pepper, or eggs for garnish, make it your own\!

Ingredients for bread:\
2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast (9 grams)\
1 teaspoon sugar (4 grams) (use honey if you prefer)\
1 ¼ cups warm water (300 grams)\
1 ¼ teaspoons (8 grams) kosher salt\
2 ½ to 3 ½ cups All-Purpose Flour (400 grams) plus extra for dusting

Method:

Place cod into cold water and shred into thin strands. Squeeze the cod to remove
salt and place into a bowl of fresh water. Repeat about 5-7 times and remove as
much water as possible on the last rinse.\
Dice onion, finely mince garlic and dice habanero pepper.\
Mix the onion, garlic and pepper into the cod and pour in citrus(or vinegar) and
olive oil.\
Season to taste with salt and pepper.\
Let sit for at least three hours or ideally overnight.\
Slice tomatoes, eggs, avocado or pepper and garnish over the top.\
Serve with crusty bread or boiled potatoes and enjoy\!

Bread method:\
Combine yeast, sugar, and warm water in a large mixing bowl.\
Add flour and salt to bowl. Mix until the dough starts to come together (dough
should be sticky).\
Cover bowl with a clean tea towel and let rise on the counter for about 1 hour,
until dough has doubled in size.\
When dough has risen, lightly flour table and tip the dough onto table.\
Fold in the edges until the dough feels tight.\
Flip the dough over and pull it into a round loaf.\
Flour a bowl and place dough in for 30 minutes to rest while your oven
preheats.\
Preheat oven to 450 degrees Fahrenheit with Dutch oven or cast iron. Place dough
into Dutch oven seam side up and bake for 30 minutes. Remove lid and bake for a
further 10 minutes or until golden brown on top.

# Cinnamon Buns

Ingredients\
Dough:\
4 cups (580g) all-purpose flour\
½ cup (107g) granulated sugar\
¾ tsp (6g) fine sea salt\
⅓ cup (84g) unsalted butter, softened\
1 cup (235g) lukewarm whole milk\
2.25 tsp (7g ) instant yeast\
2 large eggs\
1 large egg yolk

Filling:\
1 cup (225g) Demerara sugar\
2.5 Tbsp (17g) ground cinnamon\
⅓ cup (84g) unsalted butter, softened

Glaze:\
4 oz (115g) cream cheese, softened\
¾ cup (90g) powdered sugar\
2-3 Tbsp whole milk\
½ vanilla bean, beans scraped from pod

Directions\
Dough:\
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt until evenly
combined.\
Add in the softened butter and use two forks or your fingers to evenly
incorporate the butter into the flour.\
Warm the milk to about 100°F (38°C). Whisk the yeast into the warm milk.\
Mix together flour mixture and milk and eggs with a fork until a loose dough
forms\
Remove the dough to a lightly floured work surface. Knead for about 5-8 minutes,
or until it is completely smooth. Plop into a greased bowl and cover with
plastic wrap. Let the dough rise at room temperature until doubled in size,
about 90 minutes.

Filling:\
In a medium bowl whisk together the sugar and cinnamon until evenly combined.
Set aside.

Assembly and Baking:\
When the dough is doubled in size, lightly flour a work surface. Punch down the
dough and place on the work surface. Flatten the dough into the shape of a
rectangle and then roll the dough into a large rectangle that is about 27”
(69cm) long and ¼” thick (6mm). Keep the long edge of the dough parallel to
you.\
Spread the softened butter and sprinkle all of the cinnamon sugar evenly across
the top of the dough. Roll the dough into a log and cut into 2” rolls. Place in
baking tray and let rest for 30-45 minutes or until 1.5x their original size\
Bake in oven set to 375℉ (190℃) and bake for 15-20 minutes.\
Whisk cream cheese until fluffy then add the icing sugar and whisk until smooth.
Add milk to loosen the texture and whisk in the vanilla bean seeds\
Pour glaze on top and enjoy\!

# Pumpkin Pie

Ingredients\
Pumpkin Pie Filling:\
1 sugar pumpkin, cleaned and roasted\
(or substitute with a 15 oz can of pumpkin puree)

1 can of sweetened condensed milk

½ cup (100 g) packed brown sugar

2 whole eggs plus 1 egg yolk

½ tsp fine sea salt

1 tsp ground ginger

1½ tsp ground cinnamon

¼ tsp ground cloves

¼ tsp fresh grated nutmeg

2 tsp vanilla extract

Crust:\
2 cups (302 g) all-purpose flour

1 Tbsp (13 g) granulated sugar

1 tsp (5 g) fine sea salt

1 cup (113 g) cold unsalted butter, cubed

Ice water, as needed

Directions\
Pumpkin Puree:\
Cut the top and bottom off the pumpkins, halve, and remove all seeds.

Brush with oil and place on a foil-lined baking sheet. Roast at 400°F (205°C)
for \~40 minutes or until soft.

Remove skins and blend until smooth.

If watery, place in a pan and boil off some of the water

Pie Crust:\
Combine flour, sugar, and salt and mix in a bowl\
Add cold butter cubes and mix until pea sized pieces

Gradually drizzle in ice water while mixing until a cohesive dough forms.

Flatten into a 4” disk, wrap, and chill for 20 minutes (or longer if preparing
ahead).

Roll out on a floured surface to about ¼” thick and fit into a 9” pie dish,
leaving a slight overhang. Trim and crimp edges.

Chill again briefly to prevent shrinkage.

Filling:\
In a large bowl, whisk together:Pumpkin puree, Sweetened condensed milk, Brown
sugar, Eggs and yolk, Salt, spices, and vanilla

Mix until smooth and uniform.

Pour into the prepared crust.\
Place the pie onto a baking tray and bake the pie for 10 minutes at 400 °F (200
°C), then reduce the oven temperature to 375 °F (190 °C) and bake the pie for
another 35 to 40 minutes, until the pumpkin filling is set, but still has a
little jiggle to it in the centre. Cool the pie to room temperature, then chill
completely, about 4 hours, before serving.

Cool completely before slicing.\
Serving:\
Top with whipped cream and a light dusting of cinnamon or freshly grated nutmeg.

# Crispy Calamari

\- 2 whole squid, cut into rings\
\- Squeeze of lemon juice\
\- 3 tsp bicarbonate soda\
\- Large pinch of salt\
\- 1 egg\
\- 1 tbsp plain AP flour\
\- Panko breadcrumbs, to coat\
\- Vegetable oil, for frying

1\. Start by preparing the squid. Skin,rinse and cut the squid into rings. Place
into a bowl with the lemon juice, bicarbonate of soda, egg, flour and salt. Mix
well, then leave to tenderise for at least 1 hour in the fridge\
2\. Heat the vegetable oil to 180 C / 355 F in a heavy based pan.\
3\. Add the panko breadcrumbs to another dish and coat the squid, in batches.\
4\. Fry the squid in the oil for 3-5 minutes until crisp and golden. Season with
salt and serve hot with your favourite sauce. Enjoy\!
